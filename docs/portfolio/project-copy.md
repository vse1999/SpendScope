## Portfolio Page Summary

SpendScope is a full-stack spend management platform designed for small teams that have outgrown ad hoc expense tracking. I built it to centralize team expenses, analytics, billing, and role-aware workspace controls in one product-minded workflow. The project emphasizes trustworthy UX, server-enforced tenant boundaries, cache-aware dashboard consistency, and deployable production behavior rather than demo-only polish.

## Resume Bullet Options

## Interview Framing

### What the project is

SpendScope is not a personal budgeting toy or school-only tracker. It is a compact expense operations product for teams that need trustworthy spend visibility, billing ownership, and role-aware management.

### What it proves

- I can build a complete product, not just isolated UI screens
- I can design around real user trust issues in finance-adjacent flows
- I can implement role-aware full-stack behavior with protected server boundaries
- I can package engineering work into a credible live product and case study

### What not to say

- "It is just an expense tracker"
- "It was my final school project"
- "I mostly wanted to practice Next.js"

### Better framing

- "I built a team spend management platform for finance and ops workflows"
- "I focused on trustworthy data flows, role-aware behavior, and billing reliability"
- "I treated the deployed product as the primary proof surface and the repo as the technical deep dive"
